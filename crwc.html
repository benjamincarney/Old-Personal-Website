Clinton River Watershed

I spent the summer of 2016 interning at the Clinton River Watershed

Summer 2016

During the Summer of 2016 I interned at the Clinton River Watershed in Rochester Hills, MI as one of three Education and Research Interns. I spent the majority of my time there travelling to different schools among the Southeast Michigan area, giving presentations on storm water pollution and indicators for local stream quality to grade school and middle school children. We would also schedule field trips with these local schools and invite them to our nearby streams for a hands on approach to learning more about stream water quality. We would have students collect and analyze the presence of a wide variety of native macroinvertabrates and use this data to determine the level of pollution present among the given stream that we were studying. Our primary mission was to encourage stream quality awareness among our younger generation and to promote more sustainable day-to-day decision making on a personal level.



In addition to focusing on education, I also spent time performing individual research to gauge the level of pollution among a number of local streams. I was tasked with collecting water samples from local streams and performing tests to determine oxygen and nitrogen levels, pH levels, fecal matter content, and a variety of other markers. I would then use this data to assess stream quality on a scale of 1-100 and report my findings back to my supervisor. Based on the results from each individual study, we would then attempt to identify potential cause factors within the area.
