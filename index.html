Hello World

Prior to arriving in Ann Arbor, I had not written a single line of code in my entire life. I had always perceived programming and most IT-related work to be some sort of monotonous, obligatory chore that a small group of misfortunate individuals had to suffer through in order to keep our basic technological infrastructure operational. It didn't take long after beginning my first semester at the University of Michigan for my misguided notion of computer science to change completely.

It all started with my first "Hello World" program in EECS 183, an introductory programming course offered through the College of Literature, Science, and Arts here at the University of Michigan. I begrudgingly enrolled in the class right before my first semester in order to satisfy an LSA graduation requirement, and I assured myself that it would be the last interaction I would ever have with computer science during my college career. To my great surprise, I quickly began to the enjoy the satisfaction that I obtained from solving challenges given to me on tests and projects in EECS 183, and I was eager to continue exploring the vast world of computer science. The following semester, I decided to leap into the next phase of the computer science major. I completed EECS 280 (Programming and Intro Data Structures) and EECS 203 (Discrete Math), thus allowing me to declare a major in computer science at the end of that same school year.
