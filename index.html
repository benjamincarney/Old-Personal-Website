About me




Background

Hi there! My name is Ben.

I am currently entering my final year at the University of Michigan and will be graduating with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science in April of 2020. Prior to arriving to Ann Arbor, I had never written a single line of code in my entire life. I had always perceived programming and most IT-related work to be some sort of monotonous, obligatory chore that a small group of misfortunate souls had to suffer through in order to keep basic technological infrastructure operational. It didn't take long before beginning my first semester at the University of Michigan for my naive assumptions to be completely transformed.

It all started with my first "Hello World" program in EECS 183, an introductory programming course offered through the College of Literature, Science, and Arts here at the University of Michigan. I begrudgingly enrolled in the class during my semester in order to satisfy one of many graduation requirements set forth by the College of LSA, assuring myself it would be the only interaction I would ever have to face with computer science. As a creative mind interested in music, photography, and writing, I quickly gravitated towards computer science for the unique challenge it offered in allowing me to build my problem solving and critical thinking abilities. I began to view the discipline much differently than I had original perceived it, recognizing it more as a series of creative puzzles and riddles that required outside-the-box thinking and less of an endless task list that simply needed to be maintained.
