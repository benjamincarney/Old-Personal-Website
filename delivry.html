Delivry

iOS app developed during the Summer of 2018

April 2018 - Present

In the spring of 2018 I joined Delivry LLC, a small, Ann Arbor-based startup as one of three iOS Development Interns that would end up working on building the iOS app for the company. I entered with close to no experience in Swift or iOS development but quickly became familiar with the conventions necessary to building a functional, ready-for-market application.



During early development stages, I primarily worked on creating the user interface. My tasks included building out login and registration views, password reset views, payment views, and a number of other frontend elements. Along the way I incorporated a number of outside APIs including: Alamofire, Stripe, GooglePlaces, and a few other small-scale, open source projects. In order to avoid conflicts with version control, our team avoided the use of storyboards in Xcode and instead built the app entirely programmatically. As a result, I became familiar with placing UI Elements using auto layout design constraints, allowing for a responsive presentation no matter which platform the app would be presented on.



Toward the later stages of development, I began connecting the frontend interface with our backend server, which was written in PHP and SQL. I was tasked with establishing a number of different connections, primarily with user registration and login, user password reset, and payment through the Stripe API.



The app is currently still a work in progress. I plan to continue working on the app throughout the 2018-2019 school year for an eventual release some time in the Fall of 2018.
