Garduino

An automated irrigation system for my garden

Summer of 2018

During the Spring of 2018 my brother and I decided that we wanted to build a small raised bed garden in our front yard that we could use to grow tomatoes and hot peppers. Inspired by projects we had seen online which used microcontrollers for very practical applications, my brother and I set out to create an automated irrigation system using an arduino microcontroller, a soil moisture sensor, and a motorized solenoid valve.



The goal was simple: to regulate water flow based on soil moisture content using a solenoid valve. The program we would have to write would need to read in soil moisture data, and based on that data, would result in either the opening or closing of the solenoid valve, thus allowing or preventing the flow of water to enter the garden.



We had one main water line running from a spigot on the side of our house to the connection you see above. This line supplied us with a constant stream of water that we could then regulate with the white solenoid valve. Wired to the solenoid valve and housed inside of a weather-proof container is the arduino and the various other electrical components that were involved in carrying out the actual functionality of the code that we had written. Attached on the opposite side of the solenoid valve is a soaker hose, which sat beneath the mulch in our garden and when supplied with a steady stream, slowly distributed water to the individual plants within our garden.



We wrote our program in C++ and tested and uploaded it to our Arduino board using Arduino's IDE. Click here to the view the code.
